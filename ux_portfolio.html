E-commerce Mobile App

Redesigning the shopping experience for better conversion rates and user satisfaction

Role

Lead UX Designer

Duration

3 months

Team

4 members

Problem Statement

The existing mobile app had a 15% conversion rate, well below industry standards. Users were abandoning their carts at checkout and struggling to find products efficiently. Our goal was to identify pain points and redesign the experience to increase conversions and user satisfaction.

Research & Discovery

User Interviews

We conducted 20 user interviews with existing customers to understand their shopping behaviors, pain points, and expectations. Key findings included:

  • Users found the navigation confusing
  • Checkout process was too long and complex
  • Search functionality was ineffective
  • Product information was insufficient for decision making

Analytics Review

Analysis of user behavior data revealed specific drop-off points and helped prioritize which areas needed the most attention.

Design Process

Information Architecture

Restructured the app's navigation based on user mental models and card sorting results.

Wireframing & Prototyping

Created low-fidelity wireframes for key user flows, then developed interactive prototypes for usability testing.

Visual Design

Applied the brand's visual language while improving contrast, hierarchy, and accessibility standards.

Results

  • 40% increase in conversion rate
  • 60% reduction in cart abandonment
  • 4.2 star rating in app store (up from 2.8)
  • 25% increase in average order value

Key Learnings

This project reinforced the importance of user research in driving design decisions. The most impactful changes came from addressing fundamental usability issues rather than adding new features. Regular testing throughout the process helped validate our assumptions and prevented costly mistakes.
