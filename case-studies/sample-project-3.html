Learning Platform

Educational platform redesign focused on student engagement and retention

Role

UX Designer

Duration

5 months

Team

8 members

Problem Statement

The online learning platform had a 35% course completion rate, well below industry standards. Students were dropping out due to lack of engagement, unclear learning paths, and overwhelming content presentation.

Research & Discovery

Student Interviews

Interviewed 30 students across different demographics to understand learning preferences and barriers:

  • Students felt overwhelmed by too many options
  • Progress tracking was unclear and demotivating
  • Content felt disconnected from real-world applications
  • Social learning elements were missing

Learning Science Research

Researched cognitive load theory, spaced repetition, and gamification principles to inform design decisions.

Design Process

Personalized Learning Paths

Created adaptive learning paths based on student goals, prior knowledge, and learning pace.

Progress Visualization

Designed clear progress indicators and milestone celebrations to maintain motivation.

Social Learning Features

Integrated discussion forums, peer reviews, and study groups to create community engagement.

Results

  • 60% increase in course completion rates
  • 45% improvement in student satisfaction scores
  • 80% of students reported feeling more motivated
  • 2.5x increase in platform engagement time

Key Learnings

Personalization is crucial in education, but it must be balanced with clear structure. Students need both freedom to explore and guidance to stay on track. The most successful features were those that made progress visible and celebrated small wins along the way.
