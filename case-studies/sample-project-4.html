Healthcare Patient Portal

Simplifying complex medical information for patients while maintaining HIPAA compliance

Role

Lead UX Designer

Duration

6 months

Team

12 members

Problem Statement

Patients were overwhelmed by complex medical jargon and couldn't easily find critical health information. The existing portal had poor usability, leading to frequent support calls and patient frustration. The challenge was to simplify without compromising accuracy or compliance.

Research & Discovery

Patient Interviews

Conducted interviews with 25 patients of varying ages and health literacy levels:

  • Medical terminology was intimidating and confusing
  • Important information was buried in lengthy documents
  • Patients wanted to understand their health trends over time
  • Family members needed easy access for elderly patients

Healthcare Professional Input

Worked closely with doctors and nurses to understand information hierarchy and compliance requirements.

Design Process

Information Architecture

Restructured information based on patient priorities rather than medical categories.

Plain Language Design

Collaborated with medical writers to translate complex terms into patient-friendly language without losing accuracy.

Accessibility Focus

Ensured WCAG 2.1 compliance and tested with patients of varying abilities and technology comfort levels.

Results

  • 50% reduction in support tickets
  • 75% of patients found information easily
  • 40% increase in portal engagement
  • 92% satisfaction score from patients

Key Learnings

Healthcare UX requires balancing multiple stakeholder needs—patients want simplicity, doctors need completeness, and regulations demand compliance. The key was creating layered information architecture where patients could access basic information quickly but drill down for details when needed.
