Growthmancer: Transforming Learning Setbacks into Growth Opportunities

A gamified platform that helps skill-learners process negative emotions and maintain motivation through trial-and-error learning

[Hero Image: Growthmancer Platform Overview with Helper Characters]

Role

UX Designer

Timeline

8 weeks (Phase 1)

Tools

Figma, User interviews, Affinity mapping

Platform

Responsive Web Application

Overview

While trial-and-error learning is highly effective for skill development, learners frequently abandon their goals when faced with emotional setbacks from mistakes and failures. Growthmancer addresses this gap by providing a supportive, gamified environment where users can process negative emotions productively and maintain motivation throughout their learning journey.

[Image: Learning journey visualization showing emotional peaks and valleys]

The Challenge

Skill-learners value trial-and-error methods but struggle with handling negative emotions related to their failures, leading to abandonment of learning goals.

The Solution

A responsive web platform featuring personalized AI helper characters that guide users through structured lessons and reflections, transforming setbacks into growth opportunities.

Research & Discovery

Initial Research Goals

I conducted research to understand the common pain points in skill-learning experiences, focusing on three key areas:

  • Common experiences: What universal challenges do skill-learners face?
  • Learning effectiveness: Which methods work and which don't?
  • Motivation factors: What drives persistence versus abandonment?
[Image: Research methodology overview showing interview process and competitive analysis]

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed four educational platforms to identify market gaps:

  • Lesson structure: High demand for streamlined but comprehensive lesson flows
  • User input: Opportunity for platforms that directly respond to user-specific problems
  • Enjoyment: Fun, engaging experiences significantly improve retention and satisfaction

User Interviews

I interviewed 5 skill-learners across different domains to understand their learning journeys.

Key Insights

  • Trial-and-error effectiveness: All participants found hands-on experimentation most valuable for improvement
  • Emotional barriers: Users consistently struggled with negative self-talk and demotivation after setbacks
  • Self-reflection value: Taking time to process and review experiences helped internalize learnings
[Image: Affinity mapping showing 9 core themes from user research]

Synthesis: Affinity Mapping

Through affinity mapping, I identified 9 core themes from user research, with Handling Unproductive Emotions emerging as the primary challenge alongside Trial-and-Error Learning as the preferred method.

Problem Definition

Key Insight

The connection between trial-and-error learning and emotional management: Users value experiential learning but can experience debilitating emotional setbacks that derail their progress.

Point of View Statement

"I'd like to explore ways to help skill learners emotionally navigate trial-and-error learning and practice because users who experience negative emotions when faced with failures still find this process helpful for skill learning."

[Image: "How Might We" questions visualization with branching possibilities]

Ideation & Concept Development

Design Strategy

Through brainstorming sessions using analogous thinking and creative constraints, I explored various approaches to emotional support in learning contexts.

Final Concept: A gamified platform featuring personalized helper characters that users can project their struggles onto. These helpers guide users through structured learning experiences, transforming negative emotions into growth opportunities.

[Image: Helper character concept sketches showing growth from seed to full plant]

Core Features

  • Helper Creation: Users generate personalized AI characters based on their specific learning challenges
  • Lesson Structure: Bite-sized, interactive lessons focused on emotional resilience and growth mindset
  • Reflection System: Regular guided self-reflection sessions to process experiences
  • Progress Visualization: Gamified progression showing both helper and personal growth
  • Community Elements: Town feature for sharing graduated helpers and connecting with others

Information Architecture & Design System

Site Structure

I developed a comprehensive sitemap organizing the platform into five main sections: Authentication, Hub Page, Learning, Collection, and Profile & Social.

[Image: Site structure diagram showing user flows and navigation hierarchy]

Usability Testing

Testing Methodology

Conducted prototype testing with 6 users across 4 core tasks: account and helper creation, lesson completion, reflection completion, and finding previous reflections.

[Image: Usability testing setup and key findings summary]

Results

Positive Outcomes: Task completion times met targets, minimal user errors across all tasks, few points of confusion, and strong comprehension of product goals.

Areas for Improvement: Length concerns about lesson content, missing expected UI patterns, and graduation process unclear to 4/6 participants.

Key Iterations

  • Lesson Length Management: Split longer content into shorter screens with progress indicators
  • Enhanced Onboarding: Added 4-step visual tutorial explaining helper lifecycle
  • Content Optimization: Restructured content with better spacing and clearer visual hierarchy
[Image: Before and after comparison showing design improvements from testing]

Impact & Results

[Image: Usability testing results infographic]

Usability Testing Outcomes

  • Task completion rate: 100% across all core user flows
  • User satisfaction: Positive reception of concept and execution
  • Comprehension: Strong understanding of platform purpose and value proposition

Key Learnings

About the Problem Space: Emotional barriers in learning are more significant than initially understood. The connection between trial-and-error learning and emotional resilience is crucial but underaddressed.

About the Design Process: Early user testing revealed assumptions about familiar UI patterns that needed addressing. Visual metaphors for abstract concepts require explicit explanation during onboarding.

Future Opportunities

  • AI Integration: The helper concept provides a natural foundation for implementing AI-powered personalized guidance and adaptive lesson content
  • Enhanced Personalization: Further development of helper customization could deepen user emotional investment and identification
  • Community Features: The town/collection concept offers opportunities for peer support and shared learning experiences
  • Cross-Platform Extension: Mobile app development could support learning in real-time contexts where setbacks occur
[Image: Future roadmap visualization showing potential platform expansion]

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of addressing the complete user experience, including emotional aspects often overlooked in educational technology. The research-driven approach revealed that technical learning solutions miss a crucial component: helping users process the inevitable negative emotions that arise during skill development.

The gamification strategy proved effective not as entertainment, but as a sophisticated framework for emotional processing. By externalizing struggles onto a character that thrives on challenges, users can reframe their relationship with failure and setbacks.

Moving forward, I would explore more robust AI integration earlier in the process and conduct longitudinal studies to validate the long-term effectiveness of the approach. The foundation established in this phase demonstrates strong potential for meaningful impact on learning persistence and emotional resilience.
