Analytics Dashboard

Complex data visualization made simple for better decision making

Role

Senior UX Designer

Duration

4 months

Team

6 members

Problem Statement

C-level executives were struggling to make data-driven decisions due to overwhelming spreadsheets and disconnected reporting tools. The existing dashboard was cluttered, slow, and didn't provide actionable insights at a glance.

Research & Discovery

Stakeholder Interviews

Conducted interviews with 15 executives and managers to understand their decision-making process and data consumption habits:

  • Users needed quick overview of KPIs at a glance
  • Drill-down capability was essential for investigation
  • Real-time data was critical for timely decisions
  • Mobile access was increasingly important

Competitive Analysis

Analyzed leading dashboard solutions to identify best practices and opportunities for differentiation.

Design Process

Information Hierarchy

Prioritized metrics based on business impact and frequency of use, creating clear visual hierarchy.

Data Visualization

Designed intuitive charts and graphs that tell a story rather than just displaying numbers.

Interaction Design

Created seamless drill-down experiences and contextual filtering to support investigation workflows.

Results

  • 70% reduction in time to find key metrics
  • 85% user adoption within first month
  • 50% increase in data-driven decisions
  • 95% satisfaction score from executives

Key Learnings

Data visualization is not just about making things look pretty—it's about enabling understanding and action. The most successful charts were those that clearly showed trends and anomalies without requiring training to interpret.
